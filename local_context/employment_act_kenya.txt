KENYA EMPLOYMENT ACT
(Cap. 226 — Revised Edition)

PART I - PRELIMINARY

Section 1: Short title and commencement
This Act may be cited as the Employment Act and shall come into force on such
date as the Cabinet Secretary may, by notice in the Gazette, appoint.

Section 2: Interpretation
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—

"employee" means a person employed for wages or a salary and includes an
apprentice and indentured learner;

"employer" means any person, public body, firm, corporation or company who or
which has entered into a contract of service to employ any individual, and
includes the agent, foreman, manager or factor of such person, public body,
firm, corporation or company;

"wages" means remuneration or earnings however designated or calculated, capable
of being expressed in terms of money, fixed by mutual agreement or by national
laws or regulations, which are payable by virtue of a written or unwritten
contract of service by an employer to an employed person for work done or to be
done, or for services rendered or to be rendered;

"casual employee" means an employee whose terms of service are not permanent and
who is employed for a period of less than one month.

PART II - CONTRACT OF SERVICE

Section 3: Contract of service
A contract of service may be made orally or in writing, and may be expressed or
implied. An oral contract of service shall be as valid and enforceable as a
written contract.

Section 4: Duration of contract
(1) A contract of service for a period of three months or more, or a contract
of service which by its terms is to be performed partly or wholly outside Kenya
shall be in writing.
(2) Every contract of service shall, at the request of either party, be reduced
into writing.
(3) Every contract of service in writing shall be signed by the employer and
the employee, and a copy shall be retained by each party.

Section 5: Particulars of contract
Every contract of service in writing shall contain the following particulars—
(a) the name and address of the employer;
(b) the name of the employee;
(c) the nature of work to be performed;
(d) the date of commencement of the contract;
(e) the form of remuneration and manner of calculation and the interval at which
wages shall be paid;
(f) the hours of work;
(g) any other prescribed particulars.

Section 6: Prohibition of forced labour
No person shall use or assist in using compelled or forced labour.
Any employer who contravenes this section commits an offence and is liable to
imprisonment for a term not exceeding three years or a fine not exceeding one
hundred thousand shillings, or both.

PART III - WAGES AND REMUNERATION

Section 22: Minimum wage
(1) The Cabinet Secretary may, by order published in the Gazette, prescribe the
minimum wages to be paid to employees in any trade, industry, or occupation
throughout Kenya or in any specified area.
(2) No employer shall pay to an employee wages at a rate lower than that
prescribed under subsection (1) as the minimum rate applicable to that employee.
(3) Any term in a contract of service which provides for payment of wages at a
rate lower than the prescribed minimum rate shall be void and of no effect, and
the minimum rate shall apply in its place.
(4) An employer who contravenes subsection (2) commits an offence and shall be
liable to a fine not exceeding fifty thousand shillings, or to imprisonment for
a term not exceeding three months, or to both.

Section 23: Payment of wages
(1) Wages shall be paid on working days only and at or near the place of
employment, or through a financial institution or mobile money transfer platform
agreed upon in writing by the employee.
(2) Wages shall not be paid in taverns or other drinking establishments or in
stores, except in the case of employees employed therein.
(3) Wages shall be paid directly to the employee, except that where an employee
is a member of a trade union and has given written authority to the employer to
deduct union dues from wages, the employer may deduct such dues.
(4) Every employer shall provide each employee with a written pay slip at the
time of each payment of wages, showing the gross wages earned and all
deductions made.

Section 24: Deductions from wages
(1) No deductions shall be made from the wages of an employee except—
(a) deductions required or authorised by law, including income tax and
    National Social Security Fund contributions;
(b) deductions authorised in writing by the employee, not exceeding such
    amount as may be prescribed;
(c) deductions for accommodation, meals, or other amenities agreed upon in
    the contract of service, at such rates as may be prescribed.
(2) The total amount of all deductions made from an employee's wages in any pay
period shall not exceed two-thirds of the total wages due to the employee in
that pay period.

Section 25: Suspension without pay
An employer may suspend an employee without pay for a period not exceeding ten
days where the employer is conducting a disciplinary inquiry against the
employee. Suspension without pay beyond ten days requires the written approval
of the Industrial Court.

PART IV - LEAVE

Section 28: Annual leave
(1) Every employee who has completed a continuous period of twelve months
service with an employer shall be entitled to a period of leave of not less than
twenty-one working days with full pay.
(2) An employee and employer may agree to accumulate leave up to a maximum of
three years, after which leave must be taken or paid out at the employee's
regular wage rate.

Section 29: Sick leave
(1) Every employee shall, after two consecutive months of service with an
employer, be entitled to sick leave of not less than seven days with full pay
and thereafter to sick leave of not less than seven days with half pay, in each
period of twelve consecutive months of service.
(2) An employer may require an employee to produce a medical certificate from a
recognised medical practitioner before granting sick leave.

Section 30: Maternity and paternity leave
(1) A female employee shall be entitled to three months maternity leave with
full pay.
(2) A male employee shall be entitled to two weeks paternity leave with full pay
in the event of the birth of a child.
(3) A female employee shall not be required to utilise her annual leave
entitlement concurrently with maternity leave.

PART V - TERMINATION OF CONTRACT

Section 35: Notice of termination
(1) Either party to a contract of service may terminate the contract upon
giving notice to the other party.
(2) The notice period shall be—
(a) twenty-eight days where the employee is paid on a monthly basis;
(b) fourteen days where the employee is paid on a weekly basis;
(c) equivalent to the interval between wage payments for any other employee.
(3) Any notice required under this section shall be in writing.

Section 44: Summary dismissal
An employer may, without notice, dismiss an employee summarily where the
employee—
(a) without leave or other lawful cause, absents himself from the place of work;
(b) is guilty of gross misconduct;
(c) wilfully neglects to perform any work which it is his duty to perform;
(d) uses threatening or insulting language to the employer;
(e) is convicted of a criminal offence.

SCHEDULE 1 - MINIMUM WAGE ORDERS
(As Gazette Notice No. 2734 of 2023)

The following minimum monthly wages apply to employment categories across Kenya:

General Labourer (Cities and Municipalities): Ksh 15,120.00
General Labourer (Rural Areas): Ksh 10,080.00
Artisan/Skilled Tradesperson (Cities): Ksh 22,680.00
Artisan/Skilled Tradesperson (Rural): Ksh 15,120.00
Clerical/Office Worker (Cities): Ksh 30,240.00
Clerical/Office Worker (Rural): Ksh 20,160.00
Driver (Cities): Ksh 26,460.00
Domestic Worker (Cities): Ksh 15,120.00
Agricultural Worker (Large Farm): Ksh 10,080.00

These rates are subject to annual review by the Cabinet Secretary for Labour
and Social Protection following consultations with the National Wages Council.
